FAQ

Questions? Answered.

Cue is a voice AI agent for Mac and Windows. Press a hotkey, speak a command in natural language, and Cue understands what you're working on, figures out the right approach, and delivers finished results — all hands-free. It works in any app, with zero configuration.

Siri handles simple queries but can't understand your workflow or run multi-step tasks. ChatGPT lives in a browser tab, separate from what you're doing. Cue sits on your desktop, understands your active context, executes tasks directly on your computer (file management, code, terminal commands), and keeps all history stored locally.

Yes. Cue is free to get started with unlimited voice dictation and daily agent credits. No credit card required. Upgrade options for power users are coming soon.

All interaction history stays on your device — nothing is uploaded. Context is processed locally and never stored on any server. Cue is signed with our Apple Developer ID (full notarization in progress). No background processes run when idle.

Cue runs on macOS 13 Ventura or later (Apple Silicon M1–M4 and Intel), and Windows 10 (version 1809 / October 2018 Update) or later, 64-bit only. Windows ARM64 is not yet supported. Separate downloads are available for each platform.

Right-click (or Control-click) Cue in your Applications folder, choose Open, then click Open in the dialog. This only needs to be done once — subsequent launches work normally. Cue is signed with our Apple Developer ID; full notarization is in progress with Apple.

On Windows, hold Alt to dictate or double-tap Alt to activate Agent mode (vs. Fn key on Mac). First launch requires microphone permission via Windows Settings → Privacy → Microphone. Agent tasks use PowerShell commands instead of bash/AppleScript. Music playback automatically pauses during recording and resumes after.

The AI understands 20+ languages including English, Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Spanish, French, and German. Voice input currently works best in Chinese; broader STT language support is rolling out in the next update.
